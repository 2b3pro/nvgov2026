Healthcare

Opioids, rural access, and the doctor shortage.

The Stakes

Nevada consistently ranks near the bottom in healthcare quality and access. Medicaid covers nearly 29% of the state budget, and 81.6% of rural and frontier Nevadans live in federally designated Health Professional Shortage Areas. The opioid crisis has produced over $1.2 billion in settlement funds‚Äîmaking healthcare policy one of the most consequential issues in this race.

Only three of five candidates have articulated substantive healthcare positions. Governor Lombardo emphasizes enforcement and administrative reform while Attorney General Ford highlights his massive opioid settlements and ACA defense. Two Republican challengers have no documented healthcare platforms whatsoever.
